Work / Research Experience

Jewish General Hospital / Lady Davis Institute – Enger Lab
Master’s Student – Radiochemistry detector development for PET radionuclide production
  • Development of a small, portable, easy-to-use radiation detector to follow the transfer of radioactivity during the automated synthesis of PET radionuclides.
  • Used micro-surface-mount electronics, microprocessor, silicon photomultipliers, and plastic scintillation fibers to construct the prototype detector.
  • Performed Geant4 Monte Carlo simulations on various detector constructions to test the efficiency of the detector components and determine the optimal detector configuration.

BC Cancer Research Institute - QURIT Lab
Honours Thesis Student (Aug 2020-present)
  • Development of a simulation algorithm for tumor growth while considering realistic parameters, such as oxygen/glucose diffusion and consumption, cell necrosis, and angiogenesis, that affect its progress.
  • Development of an algorithm for the translation of simulated images to Positron Emission Tomography images to analyze the evolution of radiomics features.
  • Aiming to identify the ideal set of radiomic features to track the disease progression and response of tumors to treatment.
